From Phishing Attack to Trust Recovery

Mandl Cho • January 2026

The Hook

"$1.97B processed. 140M transactions. $288M in Q3 2025 alone."

RedeemSG is Singapore's national voucher platform—handling billions in government disbursements. The infrastructure works flawlessly.

But consumer satisfaction: 3.1/5

Meanwhile, merchant satisfaction: 4.2/5

This isn't a product failure—it's a trust crisis.

The Scam Mechanics

How the Attack Worked:

  1. Scammers create counterfeit RedeemSG websites (domain spoofing)
  2. Unsolicited SMS: "Claim your CDC vouchers now!"
  3. Instagram infographics mimicking official government design
  4. Fake sites ask for banking credentials + personal details
  5. Victims unknowingly hand over sensitive information

Why It Worked:

  • High-stakes policy moment (CDC vouchers = money)
  • Government branding easy to replicate
  • Users unfamiliar with official redemption flow
  • No proactive education from OGP on "how to spot fakes"

The Four Problems

Problem 1: Phishing Exposure

Fake sites, SMS scams, Instagram posts—external threats that OGP can't directly control.

Problem 2: OGP's Reactive Response

SPF issued advisories. OGP stayed silent. Users felt abandoned.

Problem 3: Login Friction

Every time users need RedeemSG: hunt for link → relogin with Singpass → wait for 2FA → finally start using. 3-5 minutes of friction per use.

Problem 4: Positioning Mismatch

Positioned as consumer product (app store, branding) but treated as policy infrastructure (no trust investment).

Problem 3: Login Friction Kills Adoption

"Every time I need to use RedeemSG, I have to:
1. Hunt for the link (where did I save it?)
2. Relogin with Singpass
3. Wait for 2FA
4. Finally start using"

This 3-5 minute friction happens EVERY TIME.

For a tool used monthly (voucher redemption), this is a massive adoption barrier.

Why This Matters:

  • Compounding friction: Trust crisis + login friction = double barrier
  • Monthly use case: Users forget the link between uses
  • Singpass overhead: 2FA adds 2-3 minutes every time
  • Mobile-first: Link hunting is harder on mobile

Root Cause: 5 Whys

1. Why did users lose trust in RedeemSG?

Because they saw multiple police advisories about fake RedeemSG sites.

2. Why did police advisories damage trust?

Because users couldn't distinguish real from fake sites.

3. Why couldn't users distinguish real from fake?

Because OGP never proactively educated users on "how to identify the official site."

4. Why didn't OGP educate users proactively?

Because OGP's response was reactive (waiting for SPF advisories) instead of proactive.

5. Why was OGP's response reactive?

Because RedeemSG is positioned as a consumer product (requiring brand protection) but treated as policy infrastructure (no dedicated comms/trust team).

What OGP Did Wrong

2. No Security Education

  • No SMS verification: "We will NEVER ask for banking details"

3. Positioning Confusion

You can't position as a consumer product without investing in consumer trust infrastructure.

Key Insight 1: The Product Works—Trust Doesn't

$1.97B Processed
140M Transactions
4.2/5 Merchant Rating

This isn't a product problem. It's a communication and positioning problem.

The infrastructure handles billions without issues. The brand took damage from external threats.

Key Insight 2: Reactive ≠ Responsible

What Happened:

  • SPF issued advisories (reactive)
  • OGP stayed silent (passive)
  • Users felt abandoned

What Users Needed:

  • Proactive education: "How to stay safe"
  • Visible response: "We've got your back"
  • Trust signals: "This is the official site"

In a trust crisis, silence = complicity. Users need to see you fighting for them.

Key Insight 3: Merchant Side Proves the Model

Why Merchant Works (4.2/5):

  • B2B relationship (training + support)
  • Simple use case (scan QR, confirm)
  • Low phishing exposure (in-person)

Opportunity: Double down on merchant experience. Let consumer side be policy infrastructure.

Key Insight 4: Positioning Mismatch Kills Trust

The Trap:

  • Positioned as consumer product (app store, branding)
  • Treated as policy infrastructure (no trust investment)
  • Result: Unrealistic expectations, no resources to meet them

The Fix:

Either invest in consumer trust infrastructure OR reposition as backend infrastructure (like Singpass).
