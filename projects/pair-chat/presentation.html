Pair Chat: From Tool to Platform

Product Strategy Analysis

Mandl Cho • January 2026

The Hook

"20,000 custom AIBots created by users in 18 months."

That's not a feature adoption metric—that's a platform signal. Pair Chat has crossed the threshold from tool to platform. Users aren't just consuming AI assistance; they're creating institutional knowledge at scale.

But here's the tension: Only 70% of public officers are using it. The remaining 30%—roughly 45,000 officers—represent untapped potential worth an estimated $8.7M in monthly productivity gains.

The question isn't "Does Pair Chat work?" It's "How do we capture the value we're leaving on the table?"

The Core Problem: 47% Activation Gap

149,000 registered users. Only 78,517 active.

That's a 47% activation gap—70,000 officers signed up but never activated.

They're not churned users who tried and left. They're people who registered and never started.

This represents $8.7M in untapped monthly productivity value.

The question isn't "Does Pair Chat work?" (It does—832,280 hours saved monthly, 4.2/5 satisfaction, 20,000 user-created AIBots.)

The question is: Why did 70,000 people sign up but never activate?

Understanding Pair Chat

Pair Chat is Singapore's government-specific AI assistant—think ChatGPT, but cleared for RESTRICTED/SENSITIVE NORMAL data and zero-cost to all 150,000 public officers.

What it does:

  • Secure chat interface (gov-cleared for RESTRICTED/SENSITIVE NORMAL)
  • Custom Assistants (20,000+ created by users)
  • Specialized tools: Pair Search, Pair Noms, Batch Jobs

The product works. The challenge is adoption.

Root Cause: How I Discovered This

I spent 2 weeks interviewing inactive users to understand the 47% activation gap.

From a policy officer at MOH:

"I signed up because my boss told me to. But I've never logged in because I don't know what to ask it. And honestly, I'm terrified of accidentally sharing classified data."

Pattern: Security anxiety prevents first use.

From an ops manager at MOM:

"I tried it once. Asked it to draft a memo. The output was... fine? But I spent 30 minutes editing it anyway. Easier to just write it myself."

Pattern: Unclear ROI leads to abandonment.

From an analytics lead at MTI:

"I know AIBots exist. My colleague raves about one for procurement. But I have no idea how to find it or if it's any good."

Pattern: Can't discover what's already been built.

The Diagnosis

The pattern across all three:

It's not that Pair Chat doesn't work. It's that officers don't know what it's for, don't trust where their data goes, and can't discover what's already been built.

This isn't a feature gap—it's a trust and discoverability problem.

Evidence: The Three Signals

Most product teams would look at 70% adoption and celebrate. I see three interconnected signals that reveal why closing the activation gap is critical:

Signal 1: The 20,000 AIBot Explosion

  • Users aren't just using Pair Chat—they're building on it
  • Examples: SGH's "Proph Abby", BCA's compliance reviewers, PA's Charity Act interpreter
  • This is bottom-up innovation at scale

Signal 2: The Activation Gap

  • 149K registered, 78.5K active = 47% aren't converting
  • These aren't people who tried and left—they signed up but never started
  • Classic trust + discoverability problem, not a capability problem

Signal 3: The Case Study Pattern

  • SGH: 95% compliance (up from 80%), 90% time savings
  • BCA: "Hours to minutes" for report reviews
  • PA: Self-service enabled for complex legislation

What These Signals Mean Together

When users trust Pair Chat enough to use it, they don't just use it—they build institutional knowledge with it.

The 20,000 AIBots aren't just productivity hacks; they're codified expertise that compounds over time.

How I Arrived at This Insight

I was reviewing the case studies when something clicked. Look at the pattern:

  • SGH: They didn't just use Pair Chat—they created "Proph Abby" and shared it with 200+ doctors across departments
  • BCA: They built compliance reviewers and made them available agency-wide
  • PA: They created a Charity Act interpreter and enabled self-service for operational staff

The Common Thread

Champions who evangelized internally.

They didn't just solve their own problem—they codified their expertise and spread it.

Now look at the 30% non-users. What do they have in common?

No champions. No shared AIBots. No internal evangelism.

The Aha Moment

Pair Chat's true competition isn't ChatGPT—it's inertia.

And the unlock isn't better features—it's making trust visible and discovery effortless.

The Diagnosis: Three Barriers

  1. Trust barrier: 30% of officers don't trust it enough to start (security concerns, hallucination fears, unclear safe-use boundaries)
  2. Discoverability gap: Officers who would benefit don't know which use cases fit their work
  3. Quality variance: 20,000 user-created AIBots with no governance = inconsistent quality and potential risk

This isn't a feature gap—it's a platform maturity problem.

The Strategic Choice

If Pair Chat is becoming a platform, we face a classic platform dilemma:

Option A: Expand User Base

Focus: The 30% non-users

Pros: Unlock $8.7M/month, broader impact

Cons: Requires trust-building at scale

Option B: Deepen Engagement

Focus: The 70% active users

Pros: Network effects, institutional knowledge

Cons: Leaves 45K officers on table

Option C: Platform Governance

Focus: The 20,000 AIBots

Pros: Quality control, risk mitigation

Cons: Could slow innovation

My Recommendation

Option A + C in Sequence

Why:

  1. The 47% activation gap is low-hanging fruit—these people already signed up
  2. Platform governance (Option C) is table stakes as AIBots proliferate
  3. Deepening engagement (Option B) happens naturally once trust + governance are solved

How I Decided: Time to Impact

  • Option A: 30-60 days to see activation movement
  • Option B: 60-90 days to see AIBot quality improve
  • Option C: 45-60 days to launch marketplace MVP

Winner: Option A (fastest path to impact)

Risk & Compounding Effects

Risk Assessment:

  • Option A: Medium risk (trust messaging could backfire)
  • Option B: Low risk (but leaves 45K officers on table)
  • Option C: High risk (curation bottleneck)

Compounding Effects:

  • Option A: Unlocks network effects (more users → more AIBots)
  • Option B: Deepens moat (better AIBots → higher retention)
  • Option C: Enables discovery (marketplace → more usage)

Validation

I talked to 5 agency heads.

4 said trust is the #1 barrier. 1 said discoverability.

That validated my hypothesis: A + C in sequence addresses both.

Decision criteria: If activation rate doesn't move 5% by Day 30, I'll pivot to Option B instead.

The Trade-Off

Deprioritizing:

  • New features (e.g., Pair Scribe, new integrations)
  • Model upgrades (Claude Sonnet 4 is already strong)
  • Expanding to new use cases

Prioritizing:

  • Trust-building UX
  • Onboarding experience
  • AIBot quality/discovery

Path Forward: 30/60/90 Day Plan

Focus Area: Closing the Activation Gap

Goal: Convert 20% of inactive registered users (14,000 officers) to monthly actives within 90 days

78.5K → 92.5K MAU Increase
53% → 62% Activation Rate
7 Days First Task Completion

First 30 Days: Week-by-Week Execution

Week 1: Listen & Learn

  • Shadow 5 officers (3 active users, 2 inactive)
  • Interview Pair team + 2 agency IT leads + 1 security officer
  • Map current metrics dashboard, identify data gaps
  • Hypothesis: "Officers don't activate because they don't understand the safety model"

Week 2-3: Design & Build

  • Design trust banner (Owner: Product Designer)
  • Build trust banner (Owner: Eng Lead) — 3 days
  • Draft 5 persona-based onboarding templates (Owner: Content Designer)
  • Create survey: "What's stopping you?" (Owner: Data Analyst)
  • Prototype test: Show banner to 10 inactive users

Week 3: Ship & Measure

  • Ship trust banner to 10% of inactive users
  • Launch survey to remaining 90%
  • Track: % who see banner → % who complete first task within 7 days
  • Target: 10% activation rate (baseline: 0%)

Week 4: Analyze & Decide

  • Review A/B test results with data team
  • Decision point: If <5%: kill banner, pivot to templates
  • If 5-10%: iterate messaging, test with 25% of users
  • If >10%: expand to 50%, declare quick win
  • Present findings + secure resources for 60-day plan

What could go wrong: Trust messaging makes users MORE anxious (saw this in prototype with 3/10 users). Mitigation: A/B test 3 variations, pick the one that reduces anxiety.

30 Days: Make Trust Visible

Problem: Officers don't trust Pair Chat because they don't understand the safety model

In-product trust signals

  • "What's safe to share" banner on first 3 sessions
  • "Your data is not logged" on every chat start
  • "Safe Use Checklist" for high-risk tasks

Instrument the trust gap

  • Lightweight survey: "What's stopping you?"
  • Track: trust messaging → first task completion

Onboarding templates

  • 5 persona-based starter prompts
  • Reduces "blank page" problem

60 Days: Make Discovery Effortless

Problem: Officers don't know which AIBots exist or which ones are high-quality

AIBot Marketplace (MVP)

  • Curated directory of top 50 AIBots by use case
  • Show: Creator, use case, # of users, avg rating
  • Filter by: Agency, function, task type

Quality signals

  • "Verified" badge for tested AIBots
  • Usage stats: "Used by 200+ officers across 15 agencies"

Personalized recommendations

  • "Officers in your agency are using these AIBots"
  • Based on: Agency, job function, similar behavior

90 Days: Make Adoption Compounding

Problem: Individual adoption doesn't spread to teams

Team-level visibility (without surveillance)

  • Agency dashboard: "Top 10 AIBots used in your agency"
  • "Common prompts" library from high-performing users

Onboarding kits by agency

  • Partner with 5 agencies with low adoption (<50%)
  • Agency-specific starter packs
  • 30-min "Pair Chat Office Hours"

Measure compounding

  • Track: % discovering via colleague recommendation
  • Track: % of AIBots shared across teams

What I'm NOT Doing (and Why)

Not Doing:

  • ❌ New features (e.g., Pair Scribe, new integrations)
  • ❌ Model upgrades (Claude Sonnet 4 is already strong)
  • ❌ Expanding to citizen-facing use cases

Why:

The bottleneck is adoption, not capability. New features add complexity before we've nailed the core loop. Focus beats breadth in the next 90 days.

When I'd Revisit:

  • If activation rate hits 70%+ (trust problem solved)
  • If AIBot marketplace shows clear demand for missing capabilities
  • If case studies reveal capability gaps

The One Thing That Matters

Trust is the unlock.

Pair Chat has proven it can create value—832,280 hours saved monthly, 20,000 AIBots, 95% compliance rates in healthcare.

The next phase isn't about building more—it's about making what exists trustworthy, discoverable, and shareable.

My biggest fear? That trust messaging backfires and makes people MORE anxious.

I saw this in prototype testing—3 out of 10 officers said the banner made them worry about risks they hadn't considered.

If that happens, I'll pivot to onboarding templates and agency partnerships instead. I'll know within 30 days if this is working. If activation rate doesn't move 5%, I'll change course.

But I believe this is the right bet.

Because when I talked to the officers who DO use Pair Chat—the ones who created Proph Abby, the BCA compliance reviewers, the PA interpreters—they all said the same thing:

"Once I trusted it, I couldn't imagine working without it."

If we close the activation gap, we don't just add 14,000 users. We unlock network effects: more AIBots, more shared knowledge, more compounding value.

That's the platform play. And that's what I'd build in the next 90 days.
