## Problem Definition

### What Happened: Jan 2025 Phishing Crisis Timeline

| Date | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Jan 3, 2025** | CDC voucher disbursement announced | High-value target for scammers |
| **Jan 10-17, 2025** | Fake RedeemSG websites appear | Users receive unsolicited SMS + Instagram posts |
| **Jan 17, 2025** | SPF issues first phishing advisory | Public awareness, but trust already damaged |
| **Jan-Feb 2025** | Multiple SPF advisories continue | Ongoing threat, no OGP counter-messaging |
| **Present** | Consumer satisfaction: 3.1/5 | Trust erosion despite product working |

### The Scam Mechanics

**Attack Vector:**
1. Scammers create counterfeit RedeemSG websites (domain spoofing)
2. Unsolicited SMS messages sent to citizens: "Claim your CDC vouchers now!"
3. Instagram infographics mimicking official government design
4. Fake sites ask for banking credentials + personal details
5. Victims unknowingly hand over sensitive information

**Why It Worked:**
- High-stakes policy moment (CDC vouchers = money)
- Government branding easy to replicate
- Users unfamiliar with official redemption flow
- No proactive education from OGP on "how to spot fakes"

### The Damage

**Quantitative:**
- Consumer satisfaction: **3.1/5** (merchant: 4.2/5)
- 1.1 point gap between merchant and consumer experience
- Multiple SPF advisories = sustained threat perception

**Qualitative:**
- User sentiment: "Appreciate vouchers, but worried about security"
- Reddit/forums: "How do I know if it's the real site?"
- Trust erosion despite core functionality working perfectly

**The Irony:**
RedeemSG's infrastructure handled $288M in Q3 2025 without issues. The product didn't failâ€”but the brand took damage from external threats.

## What OGP Did Wrong

### 1. Reactive, Not Proactive

**What Happened:**
- SPF issued advisories (Jan 17, Feb 14)
- OGP stayed silent (no counter-messaging)
- Users left to figure out "real vs fake" on their own

**What Should Have Happened:**
- **Before disbursement:** In-app education: "How to identify the official RedeemSG site"
- **During crisis:** Immediate public statement: "Here's how to stay safe"
- **After crisis:** Trust recovery campaign: "We've got your back"

**User Perception:**
> "The government warns me about fake sites, but RedeemSG doesn't tell me how to stay safe. Are they even aware?"

---

### 2. No Security Education

**What's Missing:**
- In-app onboarding: "How to spot fake redemption sites"
- Visual guide: "Official site vs fake site comparison"
- SMS verification: "We will NEVER ask for banking details"
- Trust signals: "Look for .gov.sg domain"

**Competitor Benchmark:**
- **Banks:** Proactive SMS: "We will never ask for your password"
- **PayPal:** In-app security center with scam examples
- **Grab:** "How to spot fake driver profiles" education

**OGP's Approach:**
- Silence. Let SPF handle it.

**Result:**
- Users associate RedeemSG with phishing risk, not security.

---

### 3. Positioning Confusion

**Current Positioning (Implied):**
- Consumer product (app store presence, branding, user-facing)
- Requires trust, brand protection, user education

**Actual Treatment:**
- Policy infrastructure (no dedicated comms, reactive support)
- Treated like GoWhere (uptime matters, trust doesn't)

**The Mismatch:**
You can't position as a consumer product without investing in consumer trust infrastructure.

**What This Means:**
Either:
- **A) Invest in trust infrastructure** (proactive comms, security education, brand protection)
- **B) Reposition as policy infrastructure** (like GoWhereâ€”success = uptime, not NPS)
