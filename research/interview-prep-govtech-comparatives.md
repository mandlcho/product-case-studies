## Denmark: Agency for Digital Government (DIGST)

**Equivalent to OGP:** âœ… Direct parallel â€” centralised government digital transformation agency

**Organizational Profile:**
- **Established:** 2011
- **Size:** 400+ employees
- **Parent:** Ministry of Digital Government (reorganized December 2022)

**Public Sentiments:**
- **Global recognition:** Denmark ranks as a **world leader in e-government** across multiple UN E-Government Surveys
- **Citizen trust:** Strong public adoption driven by mandatory digital-first policies (80% digital communication target, mandatory Digital Mailbox usage since 2014)
- **Political backing:** "Extensive political backing played a crucial role in propelling cohesive digital service development"

**Known Challenges (documented in strategy updates):**
1. **Digital inclusion** â€” "user-centred digital public services that work for everyone" (implies accessibility/equity concerns identified)
2. **AI integration** â€” New focus area, suggesting prior absence in public sector workflows
3. **Cybersecurity governance** â€” Elevated to strategic priority, reflecting growing threat landscape
4. **Digital-ready legislation** â€” Ongoing challenge to ensure laws enable (not obstruct) digital service delivery

**Proven Gap-Bridging Strategies:**
1. **Mandatory digital usage enforcement** â€” Compulsory Digital Mailbox adoption increased cost efficiency and citizen digital literacy
2. **Legislative revision** â€” Removed legal obstacles (e.g., handwritten signature requirements) enabling digital-first service design
3. **Inter-agency mediation** â€” DIGST positioned as mediator negotiating alignment across fragmented government entities
4. **Budgetary leverage** â€” Placed under Ministry of Finance to align financial incentives with digital reform goals

1. **DUAL STRUCTURE WORKS:** Policy foundation + operational agency > siloed systems
